

VERY NORMAL HUMAN 
SIMULATOR
Brand-New 3+ Player MVP Game Design Document
UE5.8 Implementation-Focused Version 2
One-Sentence Pitch
One player is the Hunter, one player is secretly an Alien operating a human body, and the remaining players are 
Human civilians creating believable chaos. The Hunter uses public commands, one direct question, and one 
accusation to identify who is pretending.
Design North Star
Chase fun, memories, laughs, trolling opportunities, deception, and instant rematches. Keep every feature in 
service of: Watch. React. Lie. Accuse. Reveal.
Document goal: give enough design and technical detail to start coding in Unreal Engine 5.8 while 
keeping the MVP small enough for a 2-3 person team.

2. Why This Version Should Be More Fun
The previous design risked becoming a checklist stealth game. This version puts people at the 
centre. Human players create false positives, the Alien hides inside social chaos, and the Hunter 
creates public situations that force everyone to perform.
⦁ Friends are the chaos engine. Human players can be nervous, funny, misleading, or 
accidentally more suspicious than the Alien.
⦁ The Alien is not doing chores. The Alien is trying to survive human rituals and public pressure.
⦁ The Hunter is not scanning for a model difference. The Hunter is creating situations and 
reading reactions.
⦁ Every round should produce a story: someone froze weirdly, someone lied badly, the wrong 
person got accused, or the Alien was in plain sight.
⦁ The game is simple to explain: one person is pretending, everyone else is trying not to get 
blamed.

4. Target Player Count and Role Rules
⦁ Minimum: 3 players. One Hunter, one Alien, one Human.
⦁ Recommended: 4 players. One Hunter, one Alien, two Humans.
⦁ Maximum MVP: 6 players. One Hunter, one Alien, remaining players are Humans.
⦁ Only one Hunter is allowed per round.
⦁ Only one Alien is allowed per round.
⦁ Roles are randomly assigned every round after the host starts the game.
⦁ The same player can receive the same role again, but an optional anti-repeat rule should be 
added after core testing if players dislike repeats.
Role Randomization Guardrail
For the first implementation, use pure random with exactly one Hunter and one Alien. After playtests, add a soft 
anti-repeat option: avoid assigning the same player Hunter or Alien three rounds in a row unless player count 
forces it.

5.2 Lobby Level Flow
The lobby is a small playable level, not just a menu. This helps the game feel social before the 
round starts.
1. Player chooses Host Public, Host Private, or Join Game.
2. Host loads into the Lobby Level.
3. Friends or public players join the lobby.
4. Players can walk around, emote, test movement, and see who is connected.
5. A large physical Play button or gate exists in the lobby level.
6. Only the host can activate the Play button.
7. When the host runs to the Play button and activates it, the server locks the lobby and begins 
round loading.
8. The game transitions to a private Role Reveal screen for each player.
9. Each player sees only their own assigned role: Hunter, Alien, or Human.
10. After a short countdown, the Store Level loads and the round starts.

9. Core Mechanics
9.1 Human Body Operation
The central skill is not a task list. It is operating a human body convincingly. All non-Hunter players 
use the same movement and interaction system so the Hunter must read timing, context, and 
reaction rather than obvious role-specific powers.
⦁ Movement uses acceleration and deceleration rather than instant start/stop.
⦁ Body rotation follows movement direction with a subtle turn arc.
⦁ Camera can look independently without instantly snapping the body.
⦁ Sudden changes cause visible correction steps, but should never feel random.
⦁ Standing still is not automatically suspicious if the player uses a believable context: phone, 
8

rack, line, mirror, conversation.
⦁ Props create readable behaviour: coffee sip, phone check, shopping bag adjust, clothing 
inspect.
9.2 Public Commands
Public Commands are the Hunter’s main active mechanic. They create normal social situations 
and let the Hunter watch reactions.

Command Balance Rule
Commands should expose reaction quality, not instantly reveal the Alien. Humans can fail too. NPCs can be 
quirky. The Hunter must connect multiple signs before accusing.

system.

Comedy Rule
Act Natural should not always save the player. Sometimes it should be slightly wrong but plausible. The laugh

9.5 Suspicious Civilian
Every round should contain at least one suspicious but innocent element. This may be a Human 
player, an NPC quirk, or both.
⦁ One NPC might walk stiffly, avoid the Hunter, hold a bag awkwardly, or repeatedly change 
their mind.
⦁ This prevents the Hunter from simply picking the weirdest character.
⦁ The Alien can use suspicious civilians as cover.
⦁ Do not make suspicious civilians too extreme in public mode or deduction becomes random.

11. Hunter Design: Preventing Helplessness
Another major worry is that the Hunter may feel bullied by a lobby full of trolls. The Hunter must 
10

have strong but limited tools.
⦁ Freeze and Look Here create readable group reactions.
⦁ The direct question lets the Hunter test one person deeply.
⦁ Suspect marking helps the Hunter remember who looked off.
⦁ The one accusation creates tension and prevents spam.
⦁ Undercover etiquette stops the Hunter from sprinting and harassing everyone, but should not 
be a hard failure condition in MVP.
Hunter Feel Target
The Hunter should feel like a clever instigator: “I made everyone react, and now I know who is lying.”
12. Alien Design: Preventing Passivity
The Alien should not win by standing still in the corner. Public Commands, the presence of 
Human players, and the need to look socially appropriate force the Alien to participate.
⦁ No secret task list in MVP. Survival and performance are enough.
⦁ Alien can use Act Natural and quick chat like Humans.
⦁ Alien receives private alien-thought barks for tension and comedy.
⦁ Hunter commands force the Alien to react under pressure.
⦁ The Alien should be able to lie through behaviour, not through complex abilities.

18. Networking and Replication
The server must be authoritative over role assignment, commands, direct questions, accusation, 
round state, and NPC routines. Clients may predict/cosmetically play local animations, but 
gameplay decisions should validate on server.
SystemReplication Rule
RolesServer assigns. Role is revealed privately to owning client 
and stored server-side. Other clients only know role at 
reveal.
Round phaseReplicated through GameState.
TimerReplicated GameState timer or server timestamp.
Public commandsServer validates Hunter has charges, replicates command 
event to all clients.
Direct questionServer validates target and charge, sends prompt to target 
client, replicates visible interaction state.
AccusationServer validates one accusation, determines result, 
replicates reveal.
NPCsServer controls routine selection and Smart Object 
reservations. Clients receive movement/activity replication.
Quick chatServer relays line if allowed and cooldown valid.
Act NaturalClient requests, server validates context, multicast cosmetic 
montage if needed.
MVP Networking Recommendation
Start with listen-server sessions. Build all authority boundaries correctly so a dedicated server can be added later 
without rewriting the whole game.

22. Public Server Guardrails
Public lobbies create additional risk. Build private first, but design public rules early so you do not 
paint yourself into a corner.
ConcernGuardrail
Humans grief Hunter constantlyReward believable play and light errands. Track scene 
disruption for awards/rewards. Keep rounds short.
Hunter harasses everyoneLimit direct question to one, accusation to one, commands to 
two. NPCs react to aggressive Hunter movement.
Players leave when not Alien/HunterShort rounds, role randomization, Human awards, immediate 
rematch.
Voice toxicityNo public open VOIP in MVP. Use quick chat. Add 
mute/report before VOIP.
Host abusePublic lobbies need kick controls, but post-match 
migration/dedicated servers are post-MVP.
Random guessingOne accusation only. Reveal should show missed clues and 
awards, not just win/loss.
Too much chaosLimit Humans’ disruptive tools. Keep public commands 
server-paced.
